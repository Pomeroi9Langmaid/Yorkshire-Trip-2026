Itinerary & must-not-miss

Eight stops over a week — with the unmissable detours marked along the way. Most are only minutes off the route, and mid-to-late August brings the moors into full purple heather bloom.

★ Must not miss

York Minster & the Shambles

One of Europe’s great Gothic cathedrals towering over a tangle of medieval lanes. The Shambles — all leaning, timber-framed shopfronts almost touching overhead — is widely said to have inspired Diagon Alley.

Minster £16 (SEK 205)Cathedral

JORVIK Viking Centre

York was Viking “Jorvik.” This centre rebuilds the 10th-century Norse city right over the dig that uncovered it — a ride through sights, sounds (and smells) of Lisa’s Scandinavian forebears who once ran this town.

★
★ Must not miss

Rievaulx Abbey

Just two miles from Helmsley, the most complete Cistercian abbey ruin in England rises from a wooded valley by the River Rye — soaring Gothic arches open to the sky, dissolved by Henry VIII in 1538 and adored by Romantic painters ever since. A lovely walk reaches it from Helmsley Castle.

Adult £12.70 (SEK 163) · EH freeAbbey
Learn more →

★ Must not miss

Helmsley Castle

Nine centuries in one skyline — medieval fortress, Tudor mansion and romantic ruin, with a dramatic dry moat, right in the heart of the market town.

Adult £9.50 (SEK 122) · EH freeCastle

Whitby Abbey

The haunting clifftop Gothic ruin that gave Bram Stoker his Dracula. Beneath it lie traces of St Hild’s 7th-century monastery, where the Synod of Whitby once fixed the date of Easter for the whole English church.

Adult £13.50 (SEK 173) · EH freeAbbey

🏛 English Heritage · for overseas visitors

Three EH sites on this stretch — is a pass worth it?

Your moors-and-coast leg takes in Rievaulx Abbey, Helmsley Castle and Whitby Abbey — all English Heritage.

Pay on the day: ~£35.70 each · ~£71 for two (SEK ~916)Overseas Pass 9-day: £58 each / £102 two (SEK ~1,309)Overseas Pass 16-day: £69 / £113 twoAnnual membership: ~£77 each / £149 joint

At 2026 prices, paying per site is cheapest for just these three — a pass only pays off if you add a fourth EH site (several are within reach of the route). SEK at £1 ≈ 12.83; verify on the linked official pages before booking.

Staithes

Perhaps the prettiest old fishing village on the coast — red-roofed cottages and narrow “ginnels” wrapped around a sheltered harbour, where a teenage James Cook first caught the sea-bug. Quieter than Robin Hood’s Bay.

Ride a steam train across the moors. It calls at Goathland — Hogsmeade in the first Harry Potter film and the village from Heartbeat — and runs daily through August.

★ Must not miss

Hole of Horcum

A vast natural amphitheatre scooped from the moor (legend blames a giant who flung a clod of earth at his wife). A roadside viewpoint and a glorious short walk on the Pickering–Whitby road — at its purple best in August.

FreeMoorland walk

★ Must not miss

Malham: Cove, Gordale Scar & Janet’s Foss

The Dales’ showstopper, all on one easy loop from Malham village: Janet’s Foss waterfall in a mossy dell (“foss” is the old Norse word for waterfall — one for Lisa), the towering ravine of Gordale Scar, and Malham Cove, an 80-metre curved limestone amphitheatre whose pavement top appears in Harry Potter.

FreeCove & waterfalls

A ruined 12th-century priory on a bend of the River Wharfe, with stepping-stones across the water and riverside woodland walks. Postcard Dales, and easy on the way in.
