Day 3
Wednesday 12 AugustYork → Whitby · ≈ 1h drive
The plan
  • The short hop leaves a full day — pick a Moors detour on the way (Helmsley + Rievaulx, or Hole of Horcum).
  • Aim to reach Whitby by ~16:00 for the Abbey (closes ~18:00), the 199 Steps and the harbour.
Helmsley + Rievaulx and Whitby Abbey is tight in one day — if you detour to Helmsley, do the Abbey on Thursday morning instead.
Must-see along the way
★ Must-see

Helmsley Castle

Medieval fortress, Tudor mansion and romantic ruin with a dry moat, in the market town.

£19 for two (SEK 244)
★ Must-see

Rievaulx Abbey

England’s most complete Cistercian abbey ruin, soaring arches in a wooded valley two miles on.

£25.40 for two (SEK 326)
★ Must-see

Hole of Horcum

A vast natural amphitheatre on the moor — roadside viewpoint and a short walk, purple in August.

✓ Free
★ Must-see

Whitby Abbey

The haunting clifftop Gothic ruin that gave Bram Stoker his Dracula, above the 199 Steps.

£27 for two (SEK 346)
Three English Heritage sites on this stretch. Helmsley Castle, Rievaulx Abbey and Whitby Abbey come to about £71 for the two of you. An Overseas Visitor Pass (£102 for two) only pays off if you add a 4th EH site — so for these three, pay on the day. EH Overseas Pass →
